**Executive summary:** The Double Wing is one of the best offenses you can run at 10U — it hides your
weakest kids, lets your athletes carry, teaches angle blocking instead of open-field tracking, and
wins with deception off a handful of run actions. The scheme here is sound and well-mirrored. The
problem is **breadth**: 15 formations and ~26 plays is far more than a 10U team can rep, and the
book is formation diagrams + play names with **no written blocking assignments**. Cut to one base
formation and a 12-play core, write the blocking down, and add ball-security/safety notes.

## Strengths (keep doing this)

1. **Right offense for the age.** Double Wing = power, deception, ball security, and angle blocking —
   exactly what 10U rewards. This is a strong, deliberate choice.
2. **Deception series is the heart of it and it's here.** Fake dive → keeper, with motion tags
   (3MO/4MO), is the money of the Double Wing. Excellent.
3. **Clean, mirrored numbering.** `[back][hole]`, fully symmetric left/right — kids learn one play, get two.
4. **Downhill, gap-scheme run game.** Dive / Blast / Toss / Sweep off tight splits with double-teams
   and pulls — teachable and safe.
5. **Situational packages exist.** Heavy, Goal-line, and Wedge looks are already in the book.

## Weaknesses (fix these)

1. **Formation overload.** 15 formations for 10U. Most run the same handful of plays — collapse to
   **Double Wing base + one Heavy/Goal-line** look.
2. **No written blocking assignments.** The book is alignment diagrams + a play name. The Double Wing
   *lives* on down-blocks, pulls, double-teams, and the wedge — none are written as rules. **#1 fix.**
3. **Play count too high (~26).** Many are formation duplicates of the same action (e.g., "Wing Right
   22 Dive" is "22 Dive" from another set). Collapse to ~12 core.
4. **No special teams** (punt, PAT, kickoff, return).
5. **No cadence / snap-count / motion rules** written.
6. **No ball-security or safety coaching notes** — critical at 10U (first real contact) and required
   before the wedge and toss go live.

## IQ Module Notes

**QBIQ —** Ideal 10U QB load: hand the dive, run the bootleg keeper, pitch the toss. Low mental
tax, high reward. Keep it there — cap motions so the mesh/timing stays clean.

**OLIQ —** This is the whole ballgame and it's undocumented. Double Wing is an offensive-line
offense: down blocks, gap pulls, double-teams, and the wedge. **Write one line of "who blocks whom"
under every play.** The Wedge (page 2) is a great 10U short-yardage tool — but coach it heads-up, no
launching.

**TeamIQ —** Identity is strong and consistent (power + deception). The only dilution is formation
sprawl: too many looks means too few reps per look. Fewer formations → more reps → a real edge.

**MistakeIQ —** Highest baked-in risk at 10U: **toss/pitch fumbles** (pitches are turnover-prone —
coach the pitch relationship or lean on handoff sweeps), **dive mesh exchange** fumbles, and **motion
timing**. Ball security is the #1 teach; a lost fumble erases a whole Double Wing drive.

## Safety Notes (10U tackle — enforce)

- Heads-up / shoulder-leverage tackling only. **No Oklahoma, Bull-in-the-Ring, or board drills.**
- **Wedge:** great 10U short-yardage tool, but coach it heads-up — no launching or leading with the
  crown. **Confirm your league allows the wedge** before installing (some youth leagues restrict it).
- **Ball security is the #1 teach** — four points of pressure on every dive, keeper, and toss.
- Any head-contact symptom → concussion protocol, out until cleared. Review the EAP at the first practice.
- If a play can't get 8–10 clean reps a week, it doesn't make the game plan.
